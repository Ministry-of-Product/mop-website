## The Full Story

This builder created a polished AI writing assistant with user accounts, templates, and a subscription model. The technology worked. The interface was clean. Early users gave positive feedback.

But when it came time to convert free users to paid, almost nobody did. The builder assumed the problem was the onboarding or the pricing. They spent weeks improving the user experience and A/B testing different price points.

The real problem was simpler: they never validated that people would pay for AI-generated content. They built a tool assuming demand existed, but they never tested whether people would actually exchange money for the output.

The shift in thinking: sell the output first (finished content), then build the tool. If people won't pay for the output, they definitely won't pay for a tool to create it themselves.

## Key Insight

If you're building a tool that creates something, validate that people will pay for the thing it creates before you build the tool. Otherwise you're building infrastructure for a business that might not exist.
