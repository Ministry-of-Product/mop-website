# Reference — Innovation Commons

This document contains key reference materials used to define the Innovation Commons system.

---

## 1. Big Slice / Small Slice (BSSS) — Ownership Framework

Source:
https://mattpaulin.com/product-management/startups/ownership/equity/frameworks/2025/12/21/bsss-updated-rules-and-allocation.html

### Summary

The Big Slice / Small Slice (BSSS) framework is a system for allocating ownership based on actual contribution over time.

Key concepts:

- Work is divided into **Big Slices** (major milestones or phases)
- Each Big Slice contains multiple **Small Slices** (individual contributions)
- Contributors earn ownership proportional to their impact within each phase
- Ownership is **earned, not assigned upfront**
- Ownership becomes fixed (“locked”) as milestones are completed

Purpose:

- Avoid premature equity decisions
- Align ownership with real work
- Enable fair distribution across evolving teams

In the Innovation Commons:

- BSSS is used to track contribution
- It is not legal equity until a company is formed
- It supports transition from exploration → structured ownership

## 2. Product Pathway — Product Development Model

Source:
https://mattpaulin.com/software/product-management/readiness-levels/2026/04/02/ProductPathway.html

### Summary

The Product Pathway defines the stages a product moves through from idea to a stable, scalable system.

Typical stages:

1. Idea
2. Prototype
3. Early Users
4. Revenue
5. Stable System

Key concepts:

- Each stage has different priorities
- Early stages focus on **problem clarity and usefulness**
- Mid stages focus on **user validation and engagement**
- Later stages focus on **reliability, scale, and sustainability**

Purpose:

- Provide a shared language for progress
- Prevent premature scaling or over-engineering
- Align work with the current maturity of the product

In the Innovation Commons:

- Projects are evaluated based on movement through these stages
- Expectations change as products mature
- Progress is measured by real-world signal, not internal opinion

## How These Work Together

The Innovation Commons combines these two systems:

- **BSSS → tracks who contributed and how ownership should be distributed**
- **Product Pathway → tracks what stage the product is in and what matters next**

Together they create:

- a fair system for ownership
- a clear system for progress
- a way to move from idea → company without premature structure

## Closing

These references form the operational backbone of the Innovation Commons.

They ensure that:
- work is grounded in reality

- progress is measurable and meaningful
