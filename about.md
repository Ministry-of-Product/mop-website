# About

Hi, I’m Matt Paulin. I run Ministry of Product out of Seattle.

I’ve spent 20 years founding companies, launching products, and delivering software that people actually use. I started Ministry of Product to do that work with other founders and executives — embedding as a product partner rather than a distant advisor.

The products I’ve built most recently are [TrueUp](https://trueup.pro), [SoundPoints](https://soundpoints.com), and [FortunateMatch](https://fortunatematch.com). Each one went from conversation to live product. That process — moving fast, staying grounded, and building the right thing — is what I bring to every engagement.

I’m based in Seattle and particularly enjoy working on projects that touch climate, IoT, education, and the ways AI is reshaping how software gets built.

## How I think about product work

Most product challenges aren’t actually technical. They’re about communication, focus, and judgment. I use product development — the practice of shipping, learning, and adjusting — as a tool for making organizations clearer about what they’re trying to accomplish.

I approach every engagement the way you’d climb a mountain without a map: as a team, moving together, making the best decision available with the information we have, and adjusting as we learn. I believe in shipping early, trusting teams with clarity instead of requirements documents, and measuring progress in usefulness rather than output.

OKRs have been part of my toolkit at every company I’ve led — not as administrative overhead, but as a mechanism for keeping strategy and execution connected.

## Who I work well with

Founders and executives who are clear about what they want to build but need a partner who can both shape the strategy and make it real. People who value judgment over speed, and learning over appearances. Teams where decisions actually matter to someone.

The best engagements happen when there’s real ownership, honest feedback, and space to think before committing. I’m not a good fit for organizations where the goal is activity over progress.
